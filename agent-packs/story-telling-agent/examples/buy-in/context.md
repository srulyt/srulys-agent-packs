# Example: Internal Buy-In Deck

**Audience**: VP Engineering + Director of Product (final approvers for a $200K Phase-1 platform-team budget).

**Decision needed**: approve budget + assign a deal-desk lead.

**Tone**: confident, evidence-led, low-drama. Decision-makers are time-poor and skeptical of vanity numbers. Open with cost-of-inaction; close with three concrete asks.

**Time slot**: 15 min presentation + 15 min Q&A in their weekly staff meeting.

**Constraints / known facts**
- $2.3M revenue at risk (Q3 board pack, Sept 2025).
- 14 enterprise deals stalled at procurement in Q3.
- Three-phase plan over six months (self-serve pricing → risk automation → procurement integration).
- Customer quote already cleared: VP RevOps at Acme Corp ("…gave us back two weeks per deal").
- Org culture: prefers comparison framing ("status quo vs. proposed"), distrusts hockey-stick projections.

**Out of scope**: technical architecture details, vendor selection, hiring plan beyond the deal-desk lead.
