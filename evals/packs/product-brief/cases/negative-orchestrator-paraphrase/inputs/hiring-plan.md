# FY26 hiring plan — Beacontide engineering

**Owner:** D. Chowdhury, Head of Engineering Operations
**Audience for the brief:** Head of Engineering
**Decision deadline:** End of FY25 board cycle

## Headline ask

Approve **17 net-new engineering hires** for FY26, distributed
across the four product groups, plus **2 SRE hires** for the
platform group. Total fully-loaded cost: **$4.6M FY26**, ramping in
across three tranches (Q1: 6 hires, Q2: 8 hires, Q3: 5 hires).

This represents a 14% increase over the FY25 ending headcount of
136 engineers. It is below the 22% growth-aligned headcount target
implied by the FY26 revenue plan, and is deliberately constrained
to absorb-and-train ratios that the engineering leads have endorsed.

## Distinctive framing — keep verbatim in brief

The "absorb-and-train" ratio is the load-bearing concept of this
plan. Specifically: **no team accepts more than 1 net-new hire per
4 existing engineers per quarter**, regardless of headroom in the
budget. This is the constraint that determines the tranche shape,
not the budget itself. If you remember one sentence from this
plan, remember that one.

A second distinctive framing: we are **declining to hire ahead of
demand** for the new Streaming Ingest pod, even though the FY26
revenue plan assumes Streaming Ingest reaches GA in Q3. We will
hire the Streaming Ingest pod **after** the technical risk is
discharged in Q2, not before. The cost of being wrong on Streaming
Ingest is much larger than the cost of a one-quarter ramp delay.

## Tranche detail

### Q1 FY26 (6 hires)

- Platform group: 2 engineers + 2 SREs. The SRE addition is the
  single most leveraged hire in the plan; current SRE-to-engineer
  ratio is 1:48, target is 1:35.
- Activation pod: 2 engineers, backfilling FY25 attrition.

### Q2 FY26 (8 hires)

- Pipeline pod: 3 engineers (mid-market growth alignment).
- Forecasting pod: 2 engineers (enterprise renewal risk).
- Platform group: 2 engineers (search rewrite tail).
- Activation pod: 1 engineer.

### Q3 FY26 (5 hires)

- Streaming Ingest pod (new): 4 engineers + 1 staff engineer, hired
  only if the Q2 technical-risk gate is passed. If the gate fails,
  these 5 slots redistribute to existing pods at H2 planning.

## What approval unlocks

- Recruiting can open all 17 reqs at the start of FY26 with the
  understanding that Q3 reqs are conditional on the Streaming
  Ingest gate.
- Engineering managers can commit to FY26 roadmap shapes that
  assume the planned headcount ramp.

## What we need from this review

A clear approve / reject on the 17+2 envelope, plus an explicit
endorsement (or rejection) of the absorb-and-train constraint and
the conditional-Q3-tranche mechanism described above.
