# Cassiterra customer research — feature direction for FY26

**Product:** Cassiterra (operations platform for distributed field
service teams: HVAC, elevator maintenance, commercial cleaning)
**Audience for the brief:** VP Product
**Window:** 12 customer conversations + post-renewal NPS sample

## What we heard

The dominant theme across 9 of 12 conversations was **dispatcher
cognitive load**. Specifically: dispatchers are spending the first
30–45 minutes of each shift reconstructing the state of the world
from overnight tickets, technician messages, and customer voicemail
transcripts before they can dispatch anything.

Three secondary themes appeared in 4–5 conversations each:

1. **Mobile parity.** Field technicians report that the mobile app
   is missing 3–4 specific actions (notably: parts requests and
   schedule swaps) that are available on the web app. They work
   around this by texting the dispatcher.
2. **Customer-facing visibility.** End customers (the building
   owners, not the dispatchers) increasingly expect a "where is my
   technician" view. Today this is offered by 2 of our top 3
   competitors and by us only via a clunky email link.
3. **Reporting export friction.** Operations managers export to
   Excel for monthly reviews; the export is slow and the column
   set is hardcoded.

## NPS signal

- Detractor comments (n=44 in last sample): 58% mention
  dispatch-related friction directly or indirectly.
- Promoter comments (n=71): mentions of mobile and reporting roughly
  in line with the conversation themes.

## Implication for FY26

The dispatcher-cognitive-load theme is the only theme cited at
near-saturation across the qualitative sample AND visible in the
detractor-side NPS comments. The other three are real but more
diffuse.
