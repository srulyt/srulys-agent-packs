# Customer interview notes — Heliograph docs platform

**Interviewer:** R. Okafor (Product Research)
**Window:** 6 interviews, weeks of FY25-Q3
**Segment:** Engineering managers at 50–500-person SaaS companies

## Headline finding

> "We don't have a docs problem. We have a 'where is the current
> answer' problem." — EM at a 180-person fintech

Five of six interviewees described the same workflow: ask in Slack,
get an answer that may or may not be in the docs, copy the answer
back into a wiki later (or not). Heliograph is the wiki in three of
those orgs and is partially adopted in two more.

## Quotable lines

- "If Heliograph could just tell me the page is stale, I'd trust it
  more than I trust our own ownership tags." (EM, fintech)
- "Search is fine. Freshness is what kills us." (Staff eng, dev tools)
- "We bought Heliograph for the editor. We stayed for the API. We're
  leaving for whoever solves staleness first." (Director, logistics)

## Behavioural signals

- 4 of 6 interviewees keep a "shadow doc" outside Heliograph (Notion,
  Google Doc, pinned Slack thread) for the docs they actually trust.
- 3 of 6 said their team treats the Heliograph search results page
  as "a list of things to verify", not "a list of answers".
- 2 of 6 had turned off Heliograph's weekly digest because it was
  surfacing pages they considered abandoned.

## Jobs to be done (interviewer synthesis)

1. **Know what's still true.** Freshness signals at the moment of
   reading, not at indexing time.
2. **Know who still owns this.** Ownership decay when the listed
   owner has left or rotated off the team.
3. **Know what to read next.** Lateral links between related pages
   that have been edited together historically.

## Out of scope for these conversations

We did not probe pricing sensitivity, mobile usage, or AI-assisted
authoring. Those are separate research tracks.
