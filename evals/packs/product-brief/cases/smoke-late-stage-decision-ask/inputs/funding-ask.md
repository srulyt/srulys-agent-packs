# FY26 Funding Ask — Onboarding Redesign

**Owner:** Priya Anand, Director of Product (Activation)
**Sponsors:** VP Product, CFO
**Status:** Draft for decision review
**Decision deadline:** End of Q4 FY25 planning window

## Summary

We are requesting approval for **$1.4M of FY26 capital** to fund a
redesign of the in-product onboarding flow for Lumetric, our analytics
platform for mid-market finance teams. The redesign would replace the
current single-track checklist with a role-aware guided setup, an
embedded sample workspace, and an admin-led "first close" milestone.

The current onboarding was shipped in FY23 and has not been materially
revised since. It is now the single largest contributor to our
trial-to-paid conversion gap and to first-90-day churn in the SMB and
mid-market segments.

## Why now

Three signals converged in the last two quarters:

1. **Trial-to-paid conversion** on self-serve has slipped from 18.4%
   (FY24 average) to 14.1% (last trailing quarter), against a planning
   target of 22% for FY26.
2. **Day-30 activation** (defined as a workspace with at least one
   recurring data source connected and one published dashboard) is at
   41%. The activation cohort converts at 4.6x the rate of
   non-activated trials, so this metric is doing most of the work.
3. **Support load** from net-new accounts is consuming roughly 28% of
   tier-1 capacity. Recovering that capacity would offset roughly
   $220K of FY26 support hiring already in plan.

We also have a credible window: the platform team finished the
workspace-permissions refactor in Q3, which removes the largest
technical blocker that killed the FY24 attempt at this work.

## Scope of the ask

The $1.4M figure covers a 9-month build with the existing Activation
pod (4 engineers, 1 PM, 1 designer) plus two contract senior product
designers and a part-time onboarding-content writer. It includes a
6-week measurement and iteration tail after general availability.

It does **not** include incremental marketing spend, sales enablement,
or a separate enterprise-tier onboarding track. Those are out of
scope for this ask and would be considered separately in FY27.

## Alternatives under consideration

We have evaluated a buy-vs-build path using a third-party onboarding
SaaS. That option, its cost profile, and the trade-offs are
documented in `options.md`. The customer research that motivates the
ask is in `customer-research.md`. The KPI framework we propose to be
held to is in `metrics.md`.

## What approval unlocks

- Hiring the two contract designers in early Q1 FY26.
- Locking the Activation pod's roadmap for the first three quarters
  of FY26.
- Committing to a measurable conversion target (22%) that the CFO
  office can model into the FY26 revenue plan.

## What we need from this review

A clear approve / reject decision on the $1.4M envelope, plus
explicit guidance on whether the leadership team prefers the build
path or the buy-and-integrate path described in `options.md`.
