# Concept note — Tessera assist sidebar (rough)

This is a rough thinking note. Not a proposal yet. Just want to put
the shape of an idea on paper before I forget it.

## The hunch

For Tessera (our SMB inventory tool), I keep noticing in support
tickets that customers paste the same three or four questions over
and over: "why did this SKU go negative", "what changed in the last
sync", "is this report counting returns". They're not bugs. They're
people not finding things.

What if there was a sidebar — not a chatbot exactly, more like a
"what is this view trying to tell me" panel — that surfaces the
likely interpretation of whatever the user is currently looking at?

## What I don't know yet

- How often this actually happens vs. my impression. I don't have a
  ticket-tag breakdown handy.
- Whether existing inline help or the docs site already partly
  covers this and the issue is just discoverability.
- If a sidebar is even the right shape, or whether it should be
  contextual tooltips, or a redesign of the report headers.
- Cost. No idea. Probably non-trivial if it has to read live state.

## Why I'm writing this down

Mostly so that if I bring it up in the next roadmap conversation I
have a paragraph to point at instead of restarting from scratch. I
don't want a decision on this. I just want a short synthesis to
share with myself in two weeks.
