# Polariq customer signals — A vs B

## Mid-market cohort (Feature A's primary audience)

- Post-trial survey (FY25, n=148): "no visual pipeline" cited by
  41% of lost trials. Highest-frequency reason among the top five.
- 7 of the last 12 mid-market deals lost in late stage referenced a
  competitor's canvas in the loss-reason field.
- Mid-market segment is currently 38% of new ARR, growing.

## Enterprise cohort (Feature B's primary audience)

- 6 of 11 enterprise RFPs in the last 9 months scored
  forecast-as-of as a "must-have" or weighted requirement. We
  scored 0 on those rows.
- 2 publicly-traded customers have raised forecast
  non-reproducibility in QBR. One has tied it to renewal explicitly.
- Enterprise segment is currently 22% of ARR, slower-growing but
  higher-margin.

## SMB cohort

- Neither feature is cited as a top-five gap. SMB churn drivers are
  pricing and onboarding, not pipeline visualisation or forecast
  reproducibility.

## Cross-segment notes

- Both features are net-new surface area; neither sunsets an
  existing feature.
- Both are scoped to a single pod with a known dependency profile.
- The two pods cannot be combined to ship both in parallel — the
  data-platform team is the shared bottleneck.
